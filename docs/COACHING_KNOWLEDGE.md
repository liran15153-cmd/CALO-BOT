# Coaching Knowledge

## Purpose

CALO Coach does not fine-tune a model and does not claim to be a certified trainer, physician, physiotherapist, or dietitian. The app uses a compact, source-backed coaching knowledge layer that is injected into provider chat context as `coaching_knowledge`.

The goal is better coaching behavior: safer plans, clearer progression, consistency-first adherence support, and honest nutrition uncertainty.

## Current Knowledge Scope

- Adult general wellness coaching.
- Weekly activity targets: aerobic work plus strength work.
- Resistance training basics: major muscle groups, simple routines, gradual progression, recovery, and individualization.
- Trainer skill domains: assessment questions, FITT programming, movement patterns, progressions/regressions, load management, behavior change, and referral boundaries.
- Program design variables: needs analysis, FITT-VP, exercise selection, exercise order, load/reps, volume, rest, and progression.
- Exercise science foundations: energy systems, planes of motion, joint actions/levers, force vectors, stability, fatigue, and motor-learning basics.
- Speed, agility, and plyometric programming: landing mechanics, low-level jump entry, jump progression, sprint acceleration, deceleration/change of direction, reactive agility, rest/order, impact volume, and surface selection.
- Exercise prescription principles: FITT-VP, specificity, overload, individualization, minimum effective dose, progression, recovery, and reversibility.
- Periodization: simple micro/meso-cycle thinking, stable beginner blocks, planned variation for advanced users, and log-driven reassessment.
- Program lifecycle: normal week, reassessment cadence, plateau decisions, deload, maintenance, taper, test week, and controlled exercise changes.
- Cardiorespiratory training: moderate/vigorous weekly targets, talk test/RPE intensity monitoring, base-building before intervals, and goal-specific placement around strength work.
- Cardio programming: base aerobic work, aerobic efficiency, Zone 2 progression, Zone 3/HIIT boundaries, fat-loss support, and endurance-event distribution.
- Walking/running programming: beginner run-walk, easy-run intensity, running volume progression, long-run management, intervals/hills, runner strength support, cadence/surface changes, and missed-run adjustment.
- Daily activity and NEAT coaching: step baselines, gradual step targets, movement breaks, movement snacks, post-meal walks, active errands/commute, low-impact days, fat-loss support, and calorie-burn uncertainty.
- Environmental training risk coaching: heat-load adjustment, heat acclimatization, heat-illness warning signs, AQI/air-quality modification, cold/wind-chill adjustment, and practical outdoor-session decisions.
- Warmup and mobility: general warmup, specific movement prep, useful range of motion, flexibility work, and balance/neuromotor work when relevant.
- Warmup/cooldown protocols: pulse raiser, dynamic task-specific prep, ramp sets, static stretching dosage, cooldown truthfulness, and DOMS framing.
- Mobility, flexibility, and balance programming: dynamic warmups, active mobility, static flexibility dose, neuromotor/balance progressions, desk movement resets, and older-adult fall-prevention exercise structure.
- Assessment and reassessment: initial intake, movement observation, baseline measures, and 2-4 week reassessment using logs and outcomes that change decisions.
- Assessment and tracking protocols: client intake, baseline snapshots, movement assessment, muscular-fitness testing, cardiorespiratory checks, body-composition tracking, progress reviews, and decision rules for progress/maintain/regress.
- Progress measurement protocols: goal-based metric selection, strength progress, cardio progress, body-composition trends, adherence dashboard review, and reassessment decisions.
- Field assessments: eligibility screening, 6-minute walk, 2-minute step, 30-second chair stand, 4-stage balance, TUG, and movement snapshots for personal baseline tracking rather than diagnosis.
- Goal playbooks: build muscle, improve strength, lose fat, improve endurance, improve consistency, and maintain health.
- Goal-specific programming: beginner foundation, strength, hypertrophy, muscular endurance, power, and fat-loss support with sets, reps, rest, intensity, and practical programming notes.
- Client-profile programming: beginner/returning users, intermediate/advanced users, older adults, limited-time users, limited-equipment users, and goal-specific paths for strength, hypertrophy, fat loss, and endurance.
- Weekly structure and split selection: availability-first planning, 2-3 day full-body foundations, 4-day upper/lower options, advanced push/pull/legs boundaries, and recovery spacing between repeated muscle-group loads.
- Volume and progression coaching: minimum-effective weekly set volume, hypertrophy set targets, double progression, 2-for-2 loading, RIR/RPE autoregulation, and one-variable-at-a-time progression decisions.
- Advanced strength/hypertrophy programming: volume landmarks, proximity to failure, limited failure dosage, top-set/back-off structures, specialization blocks, plateau troubleshooting, and exercise rotation versus specificity.
- Load prescription: starting load selection, RIR/RPE calibration, set-to-set adjustments, next-session load decisions, submax/e1RM estimates, and heavy-load safety gates.
- Concurrent training: blending strength and aerobic work by goal priority, same-day order, interference management, modality choice, and recovery spacing.
- Equipment substitution: preserving movement patterns across no-equipment, bands, dumbbells, machines/cables, and progression options when load is limited.
- Session structure: exercise order, rest intervals, tempo control, supersets/circuits, and warmup/ramp sets.
- Scenario adjustments: missed workout, short time, low sleep, no equipment, pain flags, and deload decisions.
- Movement-limitation adaptations: general joint pain handling, knee-sensitive lower-body training, low-back-sensitive training, shoulder-sensitive push/pull, wrist-sensitive support work, and ankle/hip mobility limits.
- Exercise-selection rules: movement patterns with examples, regressions, progressions, and safety cues.
- Anatomy and muscle-map knowledge: lower-body, push, pull, core, arms/shoulders, and program-balance mapping through practical movement patterns rather than over-precise remote claims.
- Structured exercise library: squat, hip hinge, horizontal push, horizontal pull, lunge, anti-extension core, loaded carry, vertical push, vertical pull, glute bridge/hip thrust, step-up, calf raise, and arm accessories with muscles, cues, common errors, regressions, progressions, and safety notes.
- Technique cues: squat, hinge, push, pull, and core setup/execution/common-error guidance.
- Load monitoring: external load, internal load, RPE/sRPE, sleep, fatigue, performance trends, and recovery signals.
- Program adaptation protocols: progression candidate, high effort or fatigue, performance plateau, missed sessions, exercise substitution, and return after a break.
- Population adjustments: older adults, chronic conditions, returning after a break, and limited-equipment training.
- Special-population programming: youth resistance training, pregnancy/postpartum general activity, chronic conditions/disabilities, and older-adult multicomponent training.
- Coaching instruction protocols: session flow, show-tell-do exercise teaching, cue selection, feedback frequency, technique safety checks, and client feedback loops.
- Exercise setup and environment safety: machine adjustment, rack safeties, spotting needs, simple bracing/breathing cues, stable variation defaults, switching exercises when cueing fails, and equipment misuse checks.
- Preparticipation screening: readiness questions, red flags, and referral rules for risky symptoms or medical constraints.
- Beginner/returning-user behavior: start small, make the next action achievable, and build consistency.
- Behavior-change protocols: ABC conversation, barrier problem-solving, if-then action plans, self-monitoring feedback, social support, and relapse recovery after missed workouts.
- Adherence micro-protocols: OARS-style short coaching, barrier-to-plan mapping, implementation intentions, minimum viable workouts, self-monitoring feedback, relapse recovery, and autonomy-supportive choices.
- Hebrew coaching language: natural Hebrew response structure, terminology register, plain-language autonomy support, jargon policy, and non-punitive correction patterns.
- Common fitness myth handling: spot reduction, DOMS as an unreliable quality signal, sweat as water/heat regulation rather than fat loss, fasted cardio as optional rather than superior, and strength-training bulky-fear coaching.
- Nutrition support: general fueling, protein/carbohydrate/hydration awareness, pre/post-workout timing, ranges rather than certainty.
- Practical nutrition coaching: non-clinical scope, simple plate building, protein anchors, fiber/produce habits, hydration habits, meal-prep fallback, workout-adjacent meals, and food-image uncertainty.
- Sports nutrition: protein anchors for active users, carbohydrate fueling around training, hydration awareness, and practical meal timing.
- Body composition strategy: energy balance, moderate fat-loss phases, controlled muscle-gain phases, recomposition, scale-trend interpretation, optional non-scale measures, plateau review, and maintenance phases.
- Low energy availability / REDs guardrails: under-fueling watchlist, recovery decline, menstrual/bone-stress risk, disordered-eating boundaries, and body-composition guardrails.
- Supplement education: creatine monohydrate, caffeine/pre-workout, protein powder, beta-alanine, electrolytes, low-evidence/high-risk products, and product-quality/scope boundaries.
- Body composition coaching: weight is not the only signal; pair scale trends with strength, measurements, energy, adherence, and performance.
- Readiness and recovery support: green/yellow/red-day decisions, sleep/stress adjustment, DOMS handling, rest days, and conservative handling of fatigue or pain.
- Advanced recovery/readiness protocols: sleep-debt adjustment, stress readiness, DOMS decisions, conservative return after illness, travel/maintenance weeks, overreaching watchlists, and recovery-priority ordering.
- Safety boundaries: pain, dangerous symptoms, medical conditions, extreme dieting, unsafe substances, and cases requiring qualified professional help.

## Implementation

- `backend/app/services/coaching_knowledge.py` owns the versioned knowledge context.
- `ContextBuilder` includes that context for provider-backed chat.
- `TrainingAdaptationService` summarizes recent workout logs into a compact `training_status` signal: recent completions, skips, pain flags, effort signals, and one suggested adjustment.
- `WorkoutPlanBuilder` is the deterministic saved-plan builder. It does not call AI and does not parse provider prompt summaries. It receives normalized profile/request/log inputs and returns a `StructuredWorkoutPlan` with `plan_type`, `training_split`, `experience_level`, `session_length_minutes`, `decision_inputs`, `source_refs`, day focus, estimated duration, exercise movement patterns, target muscles, progressions, regressions, alternatives, and safety notes.
- Multi-week workout plans are capped at 1-4 weeks for this product slice. Multi-week plans can replace the active current plan; `single_session` plans are saved as one-off plan objects with `duration_weeks=1`, `days_per_week=1`, one workout row, and `is_current=false` so they do not overwrite the active multi-week plan.
- The deterministic builder uses request > prompt inference > saved profile > safe default. It chooses 1-3 days as full-body, many 4-day intermediate plans as upper/lower, and avoids treating 5-7 requested days as daily hard lifting for users without enough recovery context.
- Session length trims volume: very short sessions keep fewer movement patterns, while longer sessions can include 5-6 movements. Exercise order follows power/technical work first when relevant, then compound multi-joint movements, then assistance/core.
- Recent workout logs feed the builder through `TrainingAdaptationService`: pain caution blocks hard progression, high RPE/recovery-needed or adherence-risk signals reduce one-off session volume/intensity, and stable completion allows only one small progression variable.
- Source-backed planner rules now embedded in persisted plan `source_refs`: ACSM 2026 resistance-training guidelines, CDC adult activity guidelines, NSCA resistance-training frequency, HPRC/NSCA exercise order, NASM resistance-training acute variables, Exercise is Medicine low-back-pain guidance, and Mayo Clinic exercise warning-symptom guidance.
- Provider-backed chat receives a compact projection via `for_provider_context()` so full knowledge does not become a long prompt dump.
- `for_provider_context(intent, query=...)` also performs deterministic query-aware retrieval over a whitelist of protocol tables. When the user asks a specific question, provider context can include compact `retrieved_knowledge` hits with `topic`, `guidance`, `action`, `avoid`, and `sources`.
- When no `query` is supplied, provider context keeps the existing summary-only shape. This preserves compact no-query prompts and existing local-tool behavior.
- Query retrieval prefers relevant protocol snippets over generic summaries when fitting the prompt budget; full protocol tables remain internal and are not sent to the model.
- The full context keeps richer decision data for tests and future modules; provider context gets summaries for program design, deload rules, load monitoring, population adjustments, and technique cues.
- The full `program_quality_audit_protocols` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive only compact program-audit language through `program_design_summary` for goal fit, weekly balance, movement coverage, volume/recovery, progression, exercise fit, adherence feasibility, and safety scope.
- The full `exercise_science_foundations` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive only compact energy-system, motion-plane, and load-vector language through `exercise_prescription_summary`.
- The full `speed_agility_plyometric_protocols` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive only compact jump/sprint/landing language inside `exercise_prescription_summary` so prompts do not become speed-training manuals.
- The full `program_adaptation_protocols` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive only `program_adaptation_summary` for progression, deload, plateau, missed sessions, substitutions, and return-after-break decisions.
- The full `program_lifecycle_protocols` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive lifecycle guidance through the compact `periodization_summary` instead of another prompt section.
- For workout-plan and workout-log intents, provider context also gets compact summaries for goal-specific programming, cardio programming, exercise prescription, periodization, cardiorespiratory training, warmup/mobility, and the expanded exercise library.
- The full `movement_limitation_adaptations` table stays internal; workout provider contexts receive only `limitation_adaptation_summary` so the coach can adapt exercises around common movement limits without diagnosing injuries.
- Provider context always includes a compact adherence summary for motivation and consistency questions. The richer `behavior_change_protocols` table stays internal for tests and future coaching modules.
- General-chat provider context also receives `adherence_micro_summary` for OARS-style support, barrier identification, if-then/minimum-action planning, and autonomy-supportive choices. The full `adherence_micro_protocols` table stays internal.
- The full `hebrew_coaching_language_protocols` table stays internal; provider contexts receive only compact Hebrew-language behavior rules through `coaching_behavior` so responses stay natural, short, autonomy-supportive, and careful with fitness terminology.

- `goal_specific_programming` stays in the service layer; provider context receives `goal_programming_summary` only.
- `client_profile_programming` stays in the service layer; workout provider contexts receive `profile_programming_summary` only.
- `cardio_programming` stays in the service layer; provider context receives `cardio_programming_summary` only.
- The full `walking_running_protocols` table stays internal; provider context receives only compact walk-run, easy-run, running-volume, Zone 2, Zone 3/HIIT, and hill guidance through `cardio_programming_summary`.
- The full `daily_activity_neat_protocols` table stays internal; general-chat provider context receives only compact step-baseline, sitting-break, and calorie-uncertainty language through `daily_activity_summary`.
- The full `environment_training_risk_protocols` table stays internal; general-chat provider context receives compact heat, AQI, and cold/wind-chill language through `environment_training_summary`, while workout contexts keep only one short environment cue inside `cardio_programming_summary` to preserve prompt budget.
- The full `low_energy_availability_protocols` table stays internal; general-chat provider context receives compact `fueling_risk_summary`, workout logs receive only one folded readiness/recovery cue, and meal contexts receive a compact body-composition fueling cue.
- The structured exercise library remains internal to the service; provider context receives `exercise_library_summary` only so full exercise data does not bloat prompts.
- The full `anatomy_muscle_map` table remains internal; provider context receives only compact muscle-pattern language through `exercise_library_summary` so the coach can mention quads/glutes/hamstrings, push/pull muscles, and core roles without sending an anatomy manual.
- For meal and meal-image intents, provider context also gets compact sports-nutrition and body-composition summaries. This is coaching context for better answers, not a new refusal mechanism.
- The full `body_composition_strategy_protocols` table stays internal; general-chat, meal-log, and meal-image contexts receive only compact `body_recomposition_summary` language for energy balance, cut/bulk/recomp, and trend-based progress.
- For meal and meal-image intents, provider context also gets `practical_nutrition_summary` for plate-building, protein, produce/fiber, hydration, fallback meal planning, and food-image uncertainty. The full `practical_nutrition_protocols` table stays internal.
- The full `supplement_education_protocols` table stays internal; general-chat and meal contexts receive only `supplement_education_summary` so common supplement questions get evidence-aware answers without turning prompts into a supplement manual.
- `coach_chat_prompt()` tells the model to use `coaching_knowledge` as bounded general coaching knowledge and not to claim certification.
- `WorkoutService` remains the persistence wrapper for saved plans and workout rows. It loads the profile and recent logs, delegates plan construction to `WorkoutPlanBuilder`, then persists both the plan JSON and loggable `Workout`/`WorkoutExercise` rows.
- The knowledge context is intentionally bounded by tests so it does not become a long prompt dump.

## Limits

- This is not a certification system.
- This is not clinical decision support.
- The knowledge layer can make responses more coach-like, but it does not make the product or model a legally certified trainer.
- Technique cues are coaching prompts, not remote form diagnosis. Pain, dizziness, chest symptoms, unusual breathlessness, or uncertain injury still route to safety/referral behavior.
- `training_status` is an adaptation hint for coaching decisions, not a hard blocker.
- The knowledge layer should remain compact. Do not paste long manuals into prompts.
- Add new domains only when they improve the core loop: onboarding, saved plans, logging, dashboard, safety, or cost-controlled AI behavior.
